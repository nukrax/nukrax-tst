01 — Foundation

System Architecture

🏛️

9-Pillar Confluence Engine

Every trade scores 0–10 across nine distinct pillars: HTF Bias (W1/D1/H4/H1 weighted votes), BOS/CHoCH structure, Liquidity Sweep, Order Block proximity, FVG alignment, Premium/Discount array, Displacement, CRT setup, and Price Action pattern. A minimum score of 4 is required — A+ setups score ≥ 7.

⏱️

7-Timeframe Top-Down Engine

W1 macro bias → D1 daily structure → H4 POI zones → H1 confirmation → M30 refinement → M15 trigger → M5 precision. Every timeframe runs a dedicated rescan on each new bar. M1 is the live execution canvas where CHoCH is detected and orders are fired.

🎯

Tiered 3-Position Exit System

Three sub-positions at different lot splits: 40% closes at TP1 (1R), 35% closes at TP2 (2R), and 25% runs as a free structural runner to TP3 (3.5R). After TP1, SL moves to breakeven+1 pip — the trade becomes risk-free. Trailing activates after TP2 is hit.

🔒

Institutional Risk Architecture

Hard weekly loss cap (default 4 trades), daily drawdown halt (3%), weekly drawdown halt (6%), consecutive loss streak pauses (3→session, 4→day, 5→24h), recovery mode at 10% drawdown, crisis spread closure, and adaptive lot reduction after losing streaks.

⚡

HFT Execution Design

Persistent ATR handles eliminate per-tick indicator leaks. Async order mode minimises latency. IOC fill type for immediate-or-cancel execution. Tick-based sweep detection on M1. All heavy rescans are triggered only on new bars — never on every tick — to preserve CPU efficiency.

🛡️

Session-Gated Entries

Trading is restricted to three high-probability killzones: London (07:00–10:00 GMT), London–NY Overlap (12:00–15:00 GMT), and NY Power Hour (14:00–17:00 GMT). Asian session is excluded by default — gold's liquidity pools are best exploited during the European and US sessions.

04 — Entry Logic

Entry Models

The EA runs a 9-pillar confluence score on every potential entry. Each pillar awards points independently. A trade fires only when the total score meets the minimum threshold. Click any model below to expand details.

Confluence Score Meter — 0 to 10 points
0 — Blocked 4 — Min Entry 7 — A+ Setup 10 — Max
0–3 No Trade 4–6 Standard Entry (0.75% risk) 7–10 A+ Entry (1.25% risk)
MODEL 1
Liquidity Sweep + M1 Change of Character (CHoCH)
Core ICT/SMC model · Highest-scoring entry type · Awards +4 pts combined
The primary entry model. The EA continuously scans the M15 Liquidity Pool array (equal highs = BSL, equal lows = SSL) built from session swing highs/lows with a configurable tolerance. When a M1 candle wicks through a pool and closes back inside, a sweep is registered. The sweep then starts a countdown timer (default 6 bars). Within that window, price must form a CHoCH — break above the post-sweep high (for longs) or below the post-sweep low (for shorts) — to confirm the institutional reversal. Only when both sweep AND CHoCH fire does the entry evaluation proceed.
Condition 01
Equal Highs or Lows Identified
Two or more M15 swing highs within Inp_LiqTolerance (default 3 pips) of each other form a Buy-Side Liquidity pool. Equal lows form a Sell-Side pool. Minimum 2 touches required.
Condition 02
Sweep Candle Detected on M1
A M1 bar wicks through the pool by Inp_LiqSweepMin (2 pips) to Inp_LiqSweepMax (18 pips). The candle must close back beyond the pool level — the wick is rejection evidence.
Condition 03
CHoCH Confirmation Within Timeout
Post-sweep, price must break the post-sweep swing high (long) or low (short) by at least 1 pip on M1 within Inp_CHoCHTimeout (6) bars. Failure resets the sweep state.
Condition 04
HTF Bias Alignment
The sweep direction must not contradict the combined HTF bias. A bull sweep requires combinedBias ≥ 0. A bear sweep requires combinedBias ≤ 0. Counter-trend sweeps are blocked.
MODEL 2
Order Block (OB) Entry
SMC institutional zone model · Multi-TF scanning · Awards up to +3 pts by strength
Order Blocks are scanned on five timeframes: H4, H1, M30, M15, and M5. A Bullish OB is the last bearish candle before an impulsive move higher where the next candle's body is ≥ 1.6× the OB candle's body. A Bearish OB is the last bullish candle before an impulse lower. OBs on H4 and H1 carry a 1.5× strength multiplier, making them worth more confluence points. Once price trades into an OB zone (between its high and low), the zone awards points proportional to its strength score. Mitigated OBs (price has closed through them) are automatically removed from the array.
Condition 01
Impulse Candle Required
The candle immediately after the OB candle must have a body ≥ Inp_OBImpulseMult (1.6×) times the OB candle's body. This confirms institutional displacement — not noise.
Condition 02
Unmitgated Zone Only
The OB must not have been closed through by any candle between its formation and the current bar. Once price closes beyond the OB high (bear) or low (bull), it's marked mitigated and removed.
Condition 03
Price Inside the OB Zone
Current Ask (for longs) or Bid (for shorts) must be between the OB's low and high at entry time. The closest OB by midpoint distance is selected when multiple zones overlap.
Condition 04
Directional Alignment
Bullish OBs only count for long setups. Bearish OBs only count for short setups. OBs on opposing TFs are ignored regardless of their strength.
MODEL 3
Fair Value Gap (FVG / Imbalance)
ICT imbalance model · 3-candle gap detection · Awards +2 pts
FVGs are scanned across H1, M30, M15, and M5. A Bullish FVG exists when candle[i-1].low is above candle[i+1].high — a gap the middle candle's body skipped through. A Bearish FVG is the mirror. Minimum gap size is Inp_FVGMinPips (3.0 pips) to filter noise. FVGs act as high-probability magnets. When price retraces into an FVG mid-point and there is OB/sweep confluence, the probability of continuation rises significantly. The FVG SL is placed just below the FVG low (bull) or above the FVG high (bear).
Condition 01
3-Candle Gap Formation
Candle[i-1].low > Candle[i+1].high (bullish) or Candle[i+1].low > Candle[i-1].high (bearish). The gap between the first and third candle must be ≥ Inp_FVGMinPips in size.
Condition 02
Unmitigated Gap
The gap must not have been filled. Bullish FVGs are mitigated when bid falls below the FVG low. Bearish FVGs are mitigated when ask rises above the FVG high. Mitigated zones are purged.
Condition 03
Price Within 25 Pips
The FVG mid-point must be within 25 pips of the current price to be counted. Distant unmitigated FVGs are logged but do not score points unless price returns to their vicinity.
MODEL 4
CRT — Candle Range Theory Setup
Derived from APEX HFT source · M15 range extension model · Awards +3 pts
CRT identifies when price extends beyond a defined range (typically the Asian session or prior consolidation) and then returns inside. The extension wicking outside and closing back in signals a liquidity grab and institutional reversal. The EA scans the last Inp_CRT_Lookback (5) M15 bars to establish the range high/low. If the most recent bar extended below the range low by Inp_CRT_ExtMin (3 pips) to Inp_CRT_ExtMax (22 pips) and closed back inside, a Bullish CRT is flagged. The SL is placed at the extension wick low. CRT setups carry the highest single-model score (+3 pts) because they combine a sweep AND a range return in one signal.
Condition 01
Prior Range Formation
Last Inp_CRT_Lookback (5) M15 bars define the reference range high and low. This typically captures the Asian session consolidation window.
Condition 02
Extension Within Limits
The most recent completed M15 bar must have extended beyond the range by exactly 3–22 pips. Too small = noise. Too large = breakout, not CRT setup.
Condition 03
Close Back Inside Range
After the extension, BOTH the prior bar's close AND the current bar's open must be back inside the original range. The wick represents the trap; the return confirms the rejection.
Condition 04
Active Within 10 Bars
A CRT setup expires after 10 M15 bars (150 minutes) if no trade has fired. Stale CRT signals are automatically cleared to prevent outdated entries.
MODEL 5
HTF Weighted Bias + Break of Structure (BOS)
7-TF directional engine · Macro narrative alignment · Awards up to +7 pts
The weighted HTF bias system combines four timeframes into a directional vote. W1 contributes 3 points (highest macro weight), D1 contributes 2 points, H4 contributes 1 point, and H1 contributes 1 point. A total score of ≥ +3 activates the bull bias; ≤ -3 activates the bear bias. Anything between is neutral — and neutral bias blocks all entries. BOS detection uses swing-based structure: a rolling lookback identifies the most recent swing high and low on each timeframe. When price closes above the swing high (with the low formed more recently), a Bullish BOS is registered. H1 BOS awards +2 pts; H4 and M15 award +1 pt each.
Bias Weights
W1 = 3 · D1 = 2 · H4 = 1 · H1 = 1
Total vote ≥ +3 = Bull bias confirmed. ≤ -3 = Bear bias confirmed. -2 to +2 = Neutral, no trades taken. The bias combines all four timeframes into a single directional conviction score.
BOS Logic
Swing-Point Based Confirmation
Uses a 5-bar pivot lookback to identify true swing highs and lows. A close above the prior swing high (after the low was formed more recently) constitutes a BOS, not just a single candle close above any level.
H1 CHoCH
Change of Character on H1
Detected when price closes above a 3-bar H1 high while H4 bias is bearish (flip signal), or closes below a 3-bar H1 low while H4 bias is bullish. Awards +2 pts — signals that a HTF trend shift is beginning.
MODEL 6
Premium / Discount + Displacement + Price Action
Supporting confluence pillars · Awards +1 pt each · Maximum +3 pts combined
Three additional confluence pillars each contribute +1 point. The Premium/Discount check compares current price to the midpoint of the D1 session range — longs should be below the 50% level (discount), shorts above (premium). Displacement checks whether the last M15 candle's body is ≥ 1.6× the prior candle's body in the correct direction. Price Action scans M5 for Pin Bars (lower/upper wick ≥ 2× the body), Engulfing patterns (full body overlap), and 3-Bar Reversals (two candles in prior direction, third reverses past the midpoint of bar 1).
P/D Array
Discount for Buys · Premium for Sells
D1 session range midpoint divides price into Premium (above) and Discount (below). Buying in premium or selling in discount reduces confluence score — those trades need higher overall scores to fire.
Displacement
Impulsive Move Confirmation
Last M15 candle body must be ≥ 1.6× the previous candle's body in the trade direction. Confirms institutional momentum behind the potential entry. Avoids entries in choppy conditions.
Price Action
M5 Reversal Pattern Required
Pin Bar: lower wick ≥ 2× body (bull) or upper wick ≥ 2× body (bear). Engulfing: current body engulfs prior body. 3-Bar Reversal: two bars in the prior direction, third closes past their midpoint.

Trade Flow

Every trade passes through this exact sequence on every tick. Click each step to expand the internal logic.

1

Bar Detection + Multi-TF Rescan

New bars detected on W1, D1, H4, H1, M30, M15, M5. Each triggers its own rescan function — bias updates, BOS scans, OB/FVG scans, CRT updates — only on the bar that changed.

Rescan priority: W1→macro bias | D1→daily structure + HTF levels | H4→H4 BOS + OBs | H1→H1 BOS + CHoCH + OBs + FVGs | M30→OBs + FVGs | M15→M15 BOS + OBs + FVGs + liq pools + CRT | M5→M5 OBs + FVGs + ATR + phase detection.
2

Pre-Entry Gate Check (Every Tick)

Six hard gates must all pass before sweep detection is even attempted. Any failed gate aborts the tick immediately and updates the dashboard.

↓ Click to expand details
Gate 1 — Halt check: If g_halted=true and TimeCurrent() < g_pauseUntil, exit immediately.
Gate 2 — Risk limits: CheckRiskLimits() — weekly loss count, daily loss count, daily trade count, daily DD%, weekly DD%.
Gate 3 — Session: IsKillzone() — must be in London, Overlap, or NY window (configurable).
Gate 4 — News: IsNewsBlackout() — within ±15 min of any configured news event.
Gate 5 — Spread: Current spread must be ≤ Inp_MaxSpreadPts (35 points).
Gate 6 — ATR: M5 ATR must be between Inp_ATRMinPips (6) and Inp_ATRMaxPips (90) to confirm active but not extreme conditions.
Gate 7 — Bias: g_combinedBias must be non-zero (bull or bear confirmed).
3

Liquidity Sweep Detection (Every Tick)

Scans the M15 liquidity pool array against every incoming M1 candle. If a wick hits and closes through a pool level within the configured distance, a sweep is flagged and the CHoCH timer starts.

↓ Click to expand details
Bull sweep: M1 candle wicks below SSL price by 2–18 pips AND closes above SSL. Sweep direction must align with g_combinedBias ≥ 0.
Bear sweep: M1 candle wicks above BSL price by 2–18 pips AND closes below BSL. Bias must be ≤ 0.
Post-sweep: g_sweepWick stores the wick extreme. g_sweepRef tracks the highest high (bull) or lowest low (bear) formed after the sweep — this is the CHoCH target level.
Timer: g_sweepBars increments each M5 bar. If CHoCH not confirmed within Inp_CHoCHTimeout (6) M5 bars, sweep resets.
4

CHoCH Confirmation (Every Tick, Post-Sweep)

Once a sweep is registered, every subsequent M1 close is tested against the post-sweep reference level. A confirmed CHoCH triggers the entry evaluation pipeline.

↓ Click to expand details
Bull CHoCH: M1 close > g_sweepRef + 1 pip. Confirms institutional absorption of sell-side liquidity and structural shift to upside.
Bear CHoCH: M1 close < g_sweepRef - 1 pip. Confirms absorption of buy-side liquidity and structural shift to downside.
Alert triggered: If Inp_EnableAlerts=true, an MT5 alert fires on CHoCH confirmation — useful for manual review before the EA auto-executes.
5

Confluence Score Calculation

BuildSetup() runs the full 9-pillar scoring model. Points awarded from all active signals, OB/FVG proximity, CRT, bias, BOS, P/D array, displacement, and PA. Score must reach Inp_MinConfluence to proceed.

↓ Click to expand details
Scoring breakdown (max uncapped, capped to 10):
W1 bias: +3 | D1 bias: +2 | H4/H1 bias: +1 each | H1 BOS: +2 | H4 BOS: +1 | M15 BOS: +1 | H1 CHoCH: +2 | M1 CHoCH: +2 | Sweep: +2 | OB (by strength): +1 to +3 | FVG: +2 | P/D: +1 | Displacement: +1 | CRT: +3 | PA: +1

SL derivation priority: CRT extLow/extHigh → OB boundary → FVG boundary → sweep wick → M5 swing low/high. All SLs have an Inp_SLBuffer (5 pt) buffer added beyond the structure level.
6

Lot Sizing + Order Execution (3 Positions)

Lot size calculated from balance × risk% ÷ SL distance using tick value. Three sub-orders fire simultaneously: T1 (40% at TP1), T2 (35% at TP2), T3 (25% at TP3).

↓ Click to expand details
Lot formula: riskAmount = Capital × riskPct% | valuePerPoint = (tickValue / tickSize) × point | lots = riskAmount / (SL_points × valuePerPoint)

Risk scaling: A+ setups (score ≥ 7) use Inp_APlusRisk (1.25%). Standard setups use Inp_RiskPercent (0.75%). After Inp_ReduceAfter (2) consecutive losses, risk drops to 75% of the relevant rate. In recovery mode, risk is capped at 50% of base.

Execution: trade.SetAsyncMode(true) + ORDER_FILLING_IOC for minimum latency. Three Buy/Sell calls with different TP levels. All three share the same SL. Comment string includes model name and A+/standard classification.
06 — Trade Management

Exit Logic

Once open, the trade is managed every tick through a layered exit hierarchy. The system prioritises capital protection first, then profit maximisation.

Stage 1 — TP1 (1R)

Breakeven Move

When bid (longs) or ask (shorts) reaches exactly 1R from entry, the T1 position is closed by the broker's TP order. If Inp_MoveToBreakeven=true, all remaining SLs are moved to entry price + 1 pip. The trade is now risk-free — a stop-out on the remaining positions costs zero.

Stage 2 — TP2 (2R)

Trailing Stop Activation

When price reaches 2R, T2 closes by TP order. The trailing stop activates on the remaining T3 runner. The trail uses M5 structural swing lows/highs — not a fixed pip trail — giving the runner room to breathe while locking in profit beyond the current swing.

Stage 3 — TP3 (3.5R)

Runner to Opposing Liquidity

The 25% runner targets either the next opposing liquidity pool (BSL for longs, SSL for shorts) if one is within range, or 3.5R by default. The structural trailing stop follows M5 swing points with Inp_TrailPips (10) pip cushion, exiting only when structure breaks.

Time Exit
60 min
If TP1 has not been hit within Inp_MaxTradeMins (60 minutes), the entire trade is closed at market. Prevents positions from being held through session transitions without confirmation.
Counter-Structure Exit
M5 BOS
If a M5 swing low (longs) or swing high (shorts) forms and price closes beyond it, the trade is immediately closed. Counter-structure invalidates the original entry thesis — staying in is gambling.
Pool Re-Entry Exit
Sweep Wick
If price re-enters the original sweep wick level (trades back through g_sweepWick), the trade is immediately closed. The pool was not truly swept — institutional activity did not confirm. Thesis invalidated.
Crisis Spread Exit
45 pips
If spread exceeds Inp_CrisisSpread (45 pips) at any tick while a trade is open, ALL positions are force-closed immediately. Protects against news spike slippage and requote events.
Breakeven Trigger
After 1R
SL moves to entry + 1 pip immediately when TP1 price is touched. The trade becomes guaranteed zero-loss. This fires before the T1 broker TP order resolves.
Trail Activation
After 2R
Structural trailing stop activates after TP2 is reached. Follows M5 swing lows/highs with Inp_TrailPips (10) pip buffer. Minimum profit of 2R is locked in before trail begins.
07 — Risk Control Engine

Institutional Risk Rules

The risk engine runs on every tick and is checked before every trade. It implements full prop-firm-compatible loss control with automatic pauses, recovery mode, and weekly reset logic.

★ Max Weekly Losses
4 Trades
The critical rule. After 4 losing trades in a calendar week (Mon–Fri), the EA halts and g_pauseUntil is set to Friday 23:59. Trading resumes automatically on the following Monday. An alert fires immediately.
Max Daily Losses
2 Trades
After 2 losing trades on the same calendar day, no further entries are taken until midnight server time. The daily counter resets automatically at the start of each new day bar.
Daily Drawdown Halt
3%
If equity drops 3% from the day-open balance at any point during the trading day, the EA halts immediately for the remainder of that day. Resets when a new day counter fires.
Weekly Drawdown Halt
6%
If equity drops 6% from the week-open balance at any time, the EA halts until Friday 23:59 regardless of trade count. Prevents catastrophic drawdown in hostile market conditions.
3 Consecutive Losses
4H Pause
After 3 consecutive losing trades, the EA pauses for 4 hours (one session). This prevents fighting the market when conditions are clearly unfavourable for the current setup logic.
4 Consecutive Losses
Day Pause
Four consecutive losses triggers an end-of-day pause. The EA resumes at midnight server time. A very strong signal that market conditions have shifted — review settings before next session.
5 Consecutive Losses
24H Lock
Five consecutive losing trades locks the EA for a full 24 hours with an immediate alert. This is an extreme event requiring manual review of market conditions and EA parameters before re-enabling.
Recovery Mode
10% DD
If the drawdown from the all-time equity peak exceeds 10%, recovery mode activates. Risk per trade is capped at 50% of the configured base rate until equity recovers above the threshold.
Lot Reduction
After 2 L
After Inp_ReduceAfter (2) consecutive losses, lot size is automatically reduced to 75% of the calculated size for subsequent trades, even if the weekly and daily limits haven't been reached.
Max Daily Trades
8
Hard cap of 8 trades per day regardless of win/loss. Prevents over-trading during trending or choppy conditions. Resets at midnight. Configurable via Inp_MaxDailyTrades.
Crisis Spread
45 pips
If spread exceeds 45 pips at any tick (open position or not), ALL positions close immediately. Blocks new entries until spread normalises. Protects against news spikes and broker disconnects.
Blended R:R
~2.1R
40% at 1R + 35% at 2R + 25% runner at avg 3.5R = weighted average of 2.1R on full wins. With a 50% win rate this system is mathematically profitable. A+ setups can exceed 3R blended.
