01 — Foundation

The Philosophy

🏦

Institutional Order Flow

Retail traders lose because they chase price. This EA does the opposite — it waits for institutions to reveal their hand through Break of Structure, then follows their direction with precision entries.

🎯

Confluence Over Frequency

The EA requires four independent conditions to align before entering any trade. Fewer, higher-quality signals beat a high volume of mediocre ones — every time.

🛡️

Capital Preservation First

Every trade risks exactly 1% of account balance. The partial close at 1R removes half the position and moves stop to breakeven — the trade becomes risk-free before the final target.

📐

Multi-Timeframe Alignment

Bias is set on the higher timeframe (H1). Entries are only executed on the lower timeframe (M5) when that bias is confirmed. This eliminates counter-trend noise.

02 — Entry System

Entry Logic

All four conditions must be true simultaneously. If any one fails, no trade is taken. Click each step to expand the detail.

1

HTF Break of Structure (BOS)

The H1 chart must confirm institutional directional bias before anything else is evaluated.

↓ Click to expand

Bullish BOS: The last closed H1 candle closes above the high of two bars ago.
Bearish BOS: The last closed H1 candle closes below the low of two bars ago.

This signals that market structure has shifted — institutions have broken a key level and price is likely to continue in that direction. Without BOS, no trade is armed.

BULLISH: Close[1] > High[2] BEARISH: Close[1] < Low[2]
2

Liquidity Sweep

Price must have swept equal lows (for longs) or equal highs (for shorts) before reversing — confirming institutional manipulation.

↓ Click to expand

Sweep Low (Long): The M5 candle wicks below the prior candle's low, then closes back above it. This is a stop hunt — retail short orders below the low are triggered, providing liquidity for institutions to buy.

Sweep High (Short): The M5 candle wicks above the prior candle's high, then closes back below it. Retail long stops are hunted, institutions sell into the liquidity.

SWEEP LOW: Low[1] < Low[2] AND Close[1] > Low[2]
SWEEP HIGH: High[1] > High[2] AND Close[1] < High[2]
3

Displacement / Strong Move

A strong impulsive candle must confirm the institutional push — validating that this is a genuine move, not a slow grind.

↓ Click to expand

The body of the most recent M5 candle must be at least 1.5× larger than the body of the candle before it.

This filters out indecisive, low-conviction moves. When institutions enter the market, they do so decisively — the candle body size is the fingerprint of that conviction.

Body[1] > Body[2] × 1.5
4

Order Block Retest

Price must return to the institutional Order Block zone — the last opposing candle before the displacement impulse.

↓ Click to expand

Bullish OB: The last bearish M5 candle immediately before a strong bullish candle. This is where institutions placed their buy orders.

Bearish OB: The last bullish M5 candle immediately before a strong bearish candle. This is where institutions placed their sell orders.

The EA scans the last 10 candles to find this zone. The trade fires only when the current price (ask for buys, bid for sells) is inside the OB range. This is the entry trigger.

LONG: Ask inside OB zone [OB Low → OB High]
SHORT: Bid inside OB zone [OB Low → OB High]

03 — Exit System

Exit Logic

✂️

Stop Loss — OB Low/High + Buffer

For longs: SL placed below the Order Block low with a configurable point buffer (default 10 points). For shorts: above OB high + buffer. This protects against minor wicks through the zone while keeping the stop tight and logical.

💰

Partial Close at 1R

When price moves 1× the risk distance in profit (1R), 50% of the position is closed automatically. The stop loss is then moved to the entry price (breakeven). From this point, the trade is completely risk-free.

📈

Trailing Stop — Candle Low/High

After entry, the stop loss trails to the prior candle's low (for longs) or high (for shorts) on every tick. This locks in profit as the move extends, letting winners run without a fixed ceiling beyond the initial TP.

🎯

Full TP at 2R

The remaining 50% of the position targets 2× the initial risk (2R). Combined with the partial close at 1R, the blended risk-reward is approximately 1.5R on winners — while trailing may capture more on strong moves.
