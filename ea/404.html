Core Framework

AMD — The Market's
Operating System

// Every move in the market follows Wyckoff's three-phase cycle. PHASE-404 is built entirely around identifying where in this cycle price currently sits — and only trading during the Distribution phase after Manipulation has occurred.

PHASE 01 ·· ACCUMULATION
Accumulation
Institutions silently build positions inside a range without causing significant directional movement. This is the Asia session — consolidation, tight range, low volatility.
Sideways, range-bound structure
High volume around lows (smart money buying)
Long wicks and wick rejections
False breakouts to trap retail sellers
Objective: accumulate longs, induce retail shorts
PHASE 02 ·· MANIPULATION
Manipulation
Sharp, unpredictable moves engineered to hunt liquidity. Stop hunts above/below key levels. This is where the liquidity sweep occurs — the EA's primary trigger.
Stop hunts: targeting BSL or SSL
Wick through key level, close back inside
Fake breakouts inducing premature entries
High volatility spike to confuse retail
Objective: capture liquidity before real move
PHASE 03 ·· DISTRIBUTION
Distribution
After the sweep, institutions distribute (offload) positions by selling into the rise or buying into the fall. The EA enters here — at the FVG/OB POI after MSS confirmation.
Formation of market structure shift (MSS)
Fair Value Gap (FVG) left behind by displacement
Price retraces into FVG — EA entry zone
Strong directional candles in new direction
Objective: exit profitable positions efficiently

POI Types

Fair Value Gap
Classification

// PHASE-404 recognises 8 gap types. The EA primarily trades BISI and SIBI as entry POIs. All others are tracked as context levels or filtered out.

PRIMARY · ENTRY POI
BISI
Buy Side Imbalance · Sell Side Inefficiency
Bullish FVG. Price moves up so aggressively that clean sell-side execution fails. Gap forms below price with strong buying pressure. EA uses this as bullish entry zone after SSL sweep.
Detection:
C[2].high < C[0].low
C[1] = bullish displacement candle
Gap size ≥ 2 pips minimum
PRIMARY · ENTRY POI
SIBI
Sell Side Imbalance · Buy Side Inefficiency
Bearish FVG. Price drops aggressively leaving a gap with no buy-side activity. Strong selling pressure and absent buying. EA uses this as bearish entry zone after BSL sweep.
Detection:
C[2].low > C[0].high
C[1] = bearish displacement candle
Gap size ≥ 2 pips minimum
CONTEXT LEVEL
Inverse FVG
Inverse Fair Value Gap — IFVG
When a prior upward gap gets filled by a downward move (or vice versa). The intersection candle forms a critical zone. Liquidity from both sides has been swept — strong reaction level. Used as confirmation signal.
Two opposite FVGs overlapping
Intersection = key reaction zone
Acts as hidden S/R level
WEEKLY LEVEL
NWOG
New Week Opening Gap
Gap between Friday's close and Sunday's open. Reveals weekend market sentiment. Acts as early liquidity target or springboard. Price either fills the gap or uses it as continuation support/resistance.
Friday close → Sunday open
Early-week liquidity magnet
Watch for fill or continuation
DAILY LEVEL
NDOG
New Day Opening Gap
Gap between previous day's close and new session's open (5PM EST daily reset). Tracked by EA at 17:00 EST each day. Key intraday level — price frequently returns to this zone.
17:00 EST close price
Tracked as g_NDOG_Level
Intraday magnet / reaction
TREND SIGNAL
Breakaway Gap
Breakaway / Exhaustion Gap
Forms at the start of a strong move — price explodes out of consolidation. Signals a new market phase and shift in structure. Rarely gets filled. Marks beginning of institutional trend.
Price exits range explosively
Often unfilled long-term
Confirms trend initiation
TREND CONTEXT
Measuring Gap
Continuation / Midpoint Gap
Forms during strong trends at approximately the midpoint of a move. Used to estimate final target via measured projection. Less about imbalance, more about momentum. Usually stays unfilled until move completes.
Mid-move formation
Target = gap + equal leg
Momentum confirmation
VOLUME CONTEXT
Volume Gap
Volume Imbalance Zone
Sudden shift in trading activity — volume spikes or drops significantly between candles. Signals aggressive entry or exit by large players, leaving thin liquidity zones that act as price magnets for later rebalancing.
Volume spike/drop between bars
Thin liquidity zone remains
Magnet for future rebalancing

Entry Models

Confirmation
Methods

// Confirmations are only valid AFTER a BSL or SSL has been swept. Never look for confirmation before liquidity has been hunted. The EA requires MSS as primary confirmation.

01
MSS
Market Structure Shift
The primary confirmation used by the EA. After a sweep, price must break and close above the last lower high (bullish MSS) or below the last higher low (bearish MSS) on the M5 timeframe. This signals institutional intent.
Bullish: Close[1] > last swing high on M5
Bearish: Close[1] < last swing low on M5
Must occur AFTER liquidity sweep
Required = true in EA settings
02
Breaker
Breaker Block Confirmation
An invalidated order block that now acts as the opposite support/resistance. Price sweeps deeply into a liquidity zone, breaks the level with momentum, creating a new POI from the broken structure. The broken level flips from support to resistance or vice versa.
Price breaks through OB with momentum
Broken level becomes new POI
Bullish Breaker: former bearish OB → support
Bearish Breaker: former bullish OB → resistance
03
IFVG
Inverse Fair Value Gap
When price fills an existing FVG but fails to close the imbalance fully. The unfilled portion becomes an Inverse FVG — a hidden liquidity zone with very strong reaction potential. Both sides of liquidity have been swept creating a double-validated level.
Prior FVG partially filled
Remaining portion = IFVG zone
Two opposite imbalances overlap
Often leads to sharp reversals
04
OTE
Optimal Trade Entry (Fibonacci)
Fibonacci-based zone providing ideal entry within a retracement. The 61.8%–78.6% zone is the OTE zone. The 50% level is equilibrium. Price in OTE indicates it's in a discount (for longs) or premium (for shorts) — highest probability entry area.
Swing identified from sweep to impulse
Fib drawn on that swing
OTE zone: 61.8% – 78.6%
Equilibrium: 50% (minimum entry)
05
SMT
SMT Divergence (Correlated Pairs)
When two correlated pairs (EURUSD + GBPUSD) show divergent behavior at key levels. One makes a new high while the other fails — signals smart money manipulation and high-probability reversal. The strongest confluence signal available.
EURUSD makes higher high → GBPUSD fails
= Bearish divergence (sell signal)
EURUSD makes lower low → GBPUSD holds
= Bullish divergence (buy signal)
06
OB
Order Block (Fallback POI)
Last bearish candle before a strong bullish move (bullish OB) or last bullish candle before a strong bearish move (bearish OB). Used as fallback POI when no FVG is present. The EA requires the next candle to be 1.5× the OB body size to confirm it as a valid impulse.
Bullish OB: down candle before bullish impulse
Bearish OB: up candle before bearish impulse
Impulse ≥ 1.5× OB body (ImpulseRatio)
Secondary to FVG — used as fallback
