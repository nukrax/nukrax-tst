04 — Entry System

Five Entry Models

All five models feed into a single confluence score (0–10). The EA picks the highest-scoring valid setup each cycle. A score of 5+ triggers a standard trade. A score of 8+ triggers an A+ trade with higher risk and extended targets.

Confluence Score Thresholds
0 — No Trade 5 — Standard Entry (1% risk, 2.5R) 8+ — A+ Entry (1.5% risk, 3.5R) 10
01

CRT — Candle Range Theory

Range raid and return model. +3 points when active. Highest single-model point contribution.

↓ Click to expand details

Phase 1 — Range Formation: The EA identifies a consolidation range over the last N M15 bars (default 5). The range must be at least 5 pips wide to be meaningful.

Phase 2 — Extension (Raid): Price extends beyond the range boundary by 3–20 pips (configurable). This is the stop hunt — retail stops placed outside the range are triggered.

Phase 3 — Return (Entry): Price closes back inside the range boundary and is below the 50% retracement level. This signals the raid is complete and a reversal is forming.

SL Placement: Stop loss is placed beyond the extension wick extreme + buffer. This is the tightest SL of any model.

BULL CRT: Wick below rangeLow → close above rangeLow BEAR CRT: Wick above rangeHigh → close below rangeHigh
Timeout: 8 M1 bars. Setup expires if entry not triggered.
02

TBS — Time-Based Setups

Asian range, Opening Drive Range (ODR), and session breakout models. +2 points when active.

↓ Click to expand details

Asian Range (00:00–07:00 GMT): The high and low of the Asian session are identified each day. These levels act as key liquidity pools — price typically revisits and breaks one of them during London or NY open.

Bullish TBS: Price breaks above Asian session high with bullish combined bias. Entry on the breakout with SL below the Asian range low.

Bearish TBS: Price breaks below Asian session low with bearish combined bias. Entry on the breakdown with SL above the Asian range high.

ODR (Opening Drive Range): The first M15 candle of London open (07:00 GMT) or NY open (12:00 GMT) defines the ODR. Breakout of this range in the bias direction is a TBS entry signal.

Min range size: 10 pips to qualify Best performance: London open (07:00 GMT)
03

ICT — Inner Circle Trader Models

Order Blocks, FVGs, BOS, MSS, Liquidity Sweeps, Premium/Discount Arrays. Up to +6 points combined.

↓ Click to expand details

Order Blocks (OB): The last opposing candle before a strong displacement impulse. Scanned on H4, H1, M15, M5. H4/H1 OBs carry a 1.5× strength multiplier. Price must be inside the OB zone at entry.

Fair Value Gaps (FVG): 3-candle imbalance where candle 1's high is below candle 3's low (bull) or vice versa (bear). FVGs within 20 pips of the entry price add +2 points.

Break of Structure (BOS): Confirmed close above/below a prior swing high/low on H1 or M15. H1 BOS = +2 pts, M15 BOS = +1 pt.

Liquidity Sweep + CHoCH: Price sweeps an equal high/low by 2–20 pips with a wick, closes back inside, then a CHoCH is confirmed on M1 (close above/below post-sweep swing). +4 points total.

Premium / Discount Array: Entries in Discount (below D1 midpoint) for longs, Premium for shorts. +1 pt filter.

OB Mitigation Check: OB invalidated if price fully closed through it Displacement: Candle body must be 1.8× the prior candle
04

SMC — Smart Money Concepts

CHoCH, Inducement Liquidity, Institutional OBs, Supply/Demand zones. Overlaps and extends ICT models.

↓ Click to expand details

Change of Character (CHoCH): Distinct from BOS. A CHoCH is a short-term structural flip within an opposing HTF bias — signalling a potential reversal of the current swing. H1 CHoCH adds +2 pts. M1 CHoCH post-sweep adds +2 pts.

Inducement Liquidity: The M15 liquidity pool scan identifies equal highs and equal lows (minimum 2 touches within 3 pip tolerance) in the Asian session. These are the inducement pools that get swept before the real move.

Institutional OB Strength: OBs are graded by the displacement ratio of the candle that formed after them. Stronger displacement = higher OB strength = more confluence points.

Supply & Demand Zones: Combined with H4 structure scan to identify supply zones (for shorts) and demand zones (for longs) that align with the macro bias.

CHoCH: Price closes above/below post-sweep reference + 1 pip Liq Pool: 2+ swing touches within 3 pip tolerance = confirmed pool
05

Price Action — PA Models

Pin Bars, Engulfing, Inside Bar, Morning/Evening Star, 3-Bar Reversal, Wick Rejection. Checked on M5, M15, H1.

↓ Click to expand details

Pin Bar / Hammer: Lower wick ≥ 2.5× body (bull) or upper wick ≥ 2.5× body (bear). Strength = wick/total range. Strongest confirmation at POI zones.

Engulfing Candle: Current candle body covers ≥ 60% of prior candle body in the reversal direction. Strength proportional to body ratio.

3-Bar Reversal: Two consecutive candles in one direction followed by a strong counter candle that closes above/below the midpoint of the first candle. Strength = 0.8.

Inside Bar Breakout: A candle fully contained within the prior candle's range. Breakout of the inside bar high/low in the bias direction triggers entry.

Morning / Evening Star: Three-candle pattern — large opposing candle, small-body indecision candle, strong reversal candle. Checks midpoint penetration of candle 1.

Wick Rejection: Single candle wick of 8+ pips with wick ≥ 1.5× the body. Weakest PA signal at 0.6 strength but adds confluence at key zones.

PA checked on M5 + M15 + H1 simultaneously H1 PA requires strength ≥ 0.6 to count

05 — Exit System

Trade Management

📍

Stop Loss — Structural + Buffer

SL is placed beyond the OB low/high, CRT extension wick, TBS range boundary, or recent swing low/high depending on the active entry model. A configurable 8-point buffer is added. SL must be 7–35 pips from entry — tighter or wider is auto-rejected.

💰

TP1 — 40% Close at 1R

When price moves 1× the initial risk distance in profit, 40% of the total position is closed. Stop loss is simultaneously moved to entry price + 1 pip. The trade is now risk-free for the remaining 60%.

📈

TP2 — 35% Close at 2R

At 2× risk in profit, another 35% of the original position is closed. The trailing stop is activated for the runner. At this point, 75% of the original size has been closed — only the runner remains.

🏃

TP3 Runner — Trail to Opposing Liq

The remaining 25% trails via structural trailing stop (recent M5 swing low/high). TP3 defaults to 3.5R but will snap to the nearest opposing liquidity pool if one exists within range. Trail distance: 10 pips from price.

⏱️

Time Exit — 60 Minutes

If TP1 has not been hit within 60 minutes of entry, the entire trade is closed at market. This prevents holding through session transitions or news events without any profit confirmation.

🚫

Invalidation Exits

Three invalidation conditions close the trade immediately: (1) Counter-structure BOS forms on M5 against the trade, (2) Price re-enters the swept liquidity pool wick, (3) Spread exceeds the crisis threshold (50 pips default).
