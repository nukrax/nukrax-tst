00 — Strategy Foundation

What Is ILPS?

ILPS (Institutional Liquidity Precision System) is a confluence-based HFT strategy that targets the most reliable intraday pattern in XAUUSD: the institutional liquidity sweep followed by a structural reversal. It requires a minimum of 4 out of 5 signal pillars to align before any trade is executed — eliminating noise and ensuring only high-probability setups are taken.

P1 H1 Break of Structure (BOS)
P2 M15 Liquidity Pool Identified
P3 Unmitigated Fair Value Gap
P4 Liquidity Sweep Confirmed
P5 M1 Change of Character (CHoCH)
🏛️

Institutional Logic

Built entirely around smart money behavior — stop hunts, liquidity engineering, and order block sweeps. No lagging indicators. Every decision is based on real-time price structure and institutional footprints.

⚡

Low Latency Design

All structural calculations (BOS, FVGs, liquidity pools) run at bar close to minimize CPU load. Entry triggers fire on every tick using simple lookup logic — no complex calculations during live tick processing.

🛡️

Multi-Layer Risk Control

Session limits, daily loss caps, loss-streak pauses, spread filters, news blackouts, and a recovery mode all work together to protect the account during adverse market conditions.

🎯

Tiered Profit Capture

Every trade is split into three sub-positions: 40% closed at 1:1 R:R, 35% at 1:2, and 25% trailing to the opposing liquidity pool. This maximises captured profit while securing early gains.

02 — Entry Model

The Entry Decision Engine

Every entry passes through a 14-step sequential gate system. Any failed gate immediately terminates the evaluation for that tick — keeping the EA fast and preventing false entries. The system evaluates pre-session filters on every tick, then detects sweeps and CHoCH at bar close precision.

T1
Session Window Check
Only trade during London Open (07:00–09:30) or NY Overlap (12:00–15:30) GMT.
The EA checks GMT time on every tick. If the current time falls outside both active windows, all further evaluation is skipped immediately. This filter eliminates the Asian session (accumulation — no trades), London mid (reduced), and NY close (noise from institutional book-closing). The session filter uses GMT time directly — not broker server time — ensuring DST shifts in March and October do not affect trading windows.

T2
News Blackout Filter
Block entries 15 minutes before and 30 minutes after any high-impact event.
High-impact events — FOMC, NFP, CPI, Fed Chair speeches, US GDP, ISM — are pre-loaded into the EA via input fields in MMDD-HH:MM format. During the ±15 minute blackout window: no new entries are placed and all pending orders are cancelled. The EA resumes automatically after the blackout period expires. This protects against the explosive, unreadable volatility that occurs during macro events.
↓ Click to expand
T3
Spread Filter
Spread must be ≤ 25 pips. Crisis spread ≥ 40 pips closes all open positions.
The live spread (ASK − BID) is checked on every tick. ≤ 25 pips: trading permitted. 26–39 pips: entry blocked, existing trades managed only. ≥ 40 pips: crisis condition — all open positions are closed immediately at market and the EA halts until spread normalises. XAUUSD typically trades at 10–20 pip spread during active sessions; spikes above 25 indicate a thin or pre-news market.
↓ Click to expand
T4
Daily Exposure Limits
Max 5 trades/day, max 3 in London, max 2 in NY. Daily loss cap: 3% of equity.
Trade counters are session-specific: London trades (max 3) and NY trades (max 2) are tracked separately, both contributing to a total daily cap of 5. When the 3% daily loss limit is reached, trading is halted for the remainder of the day with no override. These counters reset at midnight GMT. The session trade limit prevents overtrading in choppy conditions that often follow the initial directional move.
↓ Click to expand
T5
Loss Streak Handler
Automatic pauses after consecutive losses. 5 losses = 24-hour EA halt.
2 losses: lot size reduced by 25%. 3 losses: EA pauses for 1 full session (~4 hours). 4 losses: EA pauses for remainder of day. 5 losses: EA pauses for 24 hours and sends an alert to the operator. The streak resets after 2 consecutive winning trades. This protects capital during unfavourable market regimes where conditions temporarily break down.
↓ Click to expand
T6
Volatility Gate (ATR Filter)
M5 ATR(14) must exceed 8 pips. Below 5 pips = dead market, all entries blocked.
The EA calculates ATR(14) on M5 at every bar close. ATR > 8 pips: full trading. ATR 5–8 pips: marginal — A+ setups only with reduced lot size. ATR < 5 pips: market is dead — all entries blocked. Additionally, if the current ATR is falling below its 5-bar average (contracting volatility), lot size is reduced 50% even if above threshold. A spiking ATR (>2× its 5-bar average) triggers a 2-minute pause to filter news spikes.
↓ Click to expand
T7
H1 Directional Bias (BOS)
A confirmed H1 Break of Structure must exist. BOS direction = permitted trade direction.
The EA scans the last 50 H1 bars to identify the most recent swing high and low. A Bullish BOS occurs when H1 price closes above the prior swing high — EA will only look for long setups. A Bearish BOS occurs when H1 price closes below the prior swing low — shorts only. BOS must be confirmed by a bar body close (not a wick) to prevent false signals from intrabar spikes. If no BOS exists, the EA waits.
↓ Click to expand
T8
Liquidity Sweep Detection
Price wicks through the identified pool by 2–15 pips. Body closes back inside. Sweep confirmed.
For a Long setup: price must spike below the SSL (Sell-Side Liquidity / equal lows) by 2–15 pips, then close the candle body above the SSL level. For a Short setup: price wicks above BSL (Buy-Side Liquidity / equal highs) by 2–15 pips and closes below. A sweep wick <2 pips is too small (noise). A sweep >15 pips indicates a potential true breakout, not a sweep — this setup is rejected. Once confirmed, the sweep wick extreme is recorded as the structural SL reference.
↓ Click to expand
T9
Fair Value Gap Alignment
An unmitigated FVG must exist within 20 pips of the swept liquidity pool.
A Fair Value Gap (FVG) is identified using a 3-candle pattern on M5: for a bullish FVG, candle 3's low is higher than candle 1's high — a price gap that represents institutional imbalance. For a bearish FVG, candle 3's high is lower than candle 1's low. The EA maintains a dynamic list of up to 20 FVGs, automatically marking them as "mitigated" once price trades back through them. Only unmitigated FVGs within 20 pips of the swept pool qualify — otherwise the setup is rejected.
↓ Click to expand
T10
Change of Character (CHoCH)
After the sweep, M1 price must break the post-sweep swing high (long) or low (short).
CHoCH is the most critical signal — it confirms institutional absorption of the sweep. After an SSL sweep, the EA tracks the highest M1 high formed post-sweep. A CHoCH Long is confirmed when M1 price closes above this reference level. After a BSL sweep, CHoCH Short is confirmed when M1 price closes below the post-sweep swing low reference. CHoCH must occur within 5 M1 candles of the sweep — if it takes longer, the setup is invalidated and the EA resets.
↓ Click to expand
T11
Momentum Delta Filter
At least 2 of the last 3 M1 candles must close in the trade direction.
Instead of a lagging RSI or MACD, the EA uses a body-to-direction check on the last 3 closed M1 candles. For longs: at least 2 of 3 candles must have Close > Open (bullish body). For shorts: at least 2 of 3 candles must have Close < Open (bearish body). This confirms that momentum has genuinely shifted in the trade direction following CHoCH — not just a single aggressive candle with two wicks.
↓ Click to expand
T12
Confluence Count (4 of 5 Pillars)
Minimum 4 pillars confirmed for standard entry. All 5 for A+ entry (full 1% risk).
The EA scores each of the 5 pillars: BOS (1pt), Liquidity Pool present (1pt), Unmitigated FVG (1pt), Sweep confirmed (1pt), CHoCH confirmed (1pt). 4 pillars = standard entry at 0.5% base risk. 5 pillars + momentum confirmed + ATR active = A+ setup at 1.0% risk. If fewer than 4 pillars are active, the EA resets the sweep state and waits for a new setup. This prevents taking low-quality signals.
↓ Click to expand
T13
Position Size Calculation
SL placed 3 pips beyond sweep wick. Lot size = (Equity × Risk%) ÷ (SL pips × pip value).
The structural stop loss is placed 3 pips beyond the extreme wick of the sweep candle. The SL distance must fall within an 8–20 pip envelope — too tight risks noise stopping the trade out; too wide means the risk calculation would require an unreasonably small lot size. If the structural SL falls outside this envelope, the trade is rejected. Lot size is then calculated precisely from account equity, the chosen risk percentage, and the pip value of the instrument. Recovery mode and loss streaks reduce the risk percentage automatically.
↓ Click to expand
T14
Order Execution
Three sub-positions opened simultaneously at market for tiered TP management.
The total lot size is split into three sub-positions: TP1 (40% of lots) at 1:1 R:R, TP2 (35%) at 1:2 R:R, TP3 (25%) at the nearest opposing liquidity pool or 1:3 R:R (whichever is closer). All three are sent via OrderSendAsync() for minimum latency. The trade is tagged with the magic number, logged to journal with full detail (entry, SL, TP levels, pillar count, ATR, session), and the consumed liquidity pool and used FVG are immediately marked to prevent reuse in the same session.

03 — Exit System

Exit Logic & Trade Management

Exits are structured in three tiers — locking profit early while letting a portion of the position run to maximum potential. Every exit rule is automated with no manual intervention required.

✂️

Stop Loss — Structural Placement

SL is placed 3 pips beyond the extreme wick of the sweep candle. For longs: 3 pips below the sweep low. For shorts: 3 pips above the sweep high. SL must fall within an 8–20 pip envelope — trades outside this range are rejected before entry.

💰

TP1 — 1:1 R:R (40% of position)

The first 40% of the position is closed at exactly 1:1 risk-reward. The moment TP1 is hit, the stop loss on all remaining sub-positions is moved to entry price + 1 pip (breakeven). From this point forward, the trade cannot result in a loss.

📈

TP2 — 1:2 R:R (35% of position)

The second 35% closes at a 1:2 risk-reward target. Once TP2 is hit, the trailing stop activates on the remaining 25% runner position. The TP2 target is also aligned with the nearest unmitigated FVG midpoint when possible.

🎯

TP3 — Liquidity Target (25% runner)

The final 25% targets the opposing liquidity pool (BSL for longs, SSL for shorts) identified on M15 — or 1:3 R:R if the pool is further. A 10-pip trailing stop manages this runner, locking profit as price extends toward the target.

⏱️

Time Exit — 45 Minutes

If a trade has been open for more than 45 minutes without hitting TP1, it is closed at market. Slow-moving trades indicate weak momentum — the setup premise may have weakened or the market has shifted to ranging conditions.

⚠️

Invalidation Exits

Immediate market close if: a counter-BOS forms on M5, spread exceeds 40 pips, price re-enters the swept liquidity pool body, or an unscheduled high-impact news event begins. These exits take priority over all TP levels.

05 — Algorithmic Rule Set

Complete Rule Summary

The EA evaluates every rule sequentially on every tick. The first FAIL terminates evaluation for that tick. This ensures deterministic, unambiguous decisions with minimal processing overhead.

Pre-Session Initialization (once per session)

I-1
Load news calendar. Parse all high-impact events. Tag ±15 minute blackout windows around each event.
I-2
Scan H1 BOS. Look back 50 bars. Record BOS_DIRECTION = BULL or BEAR. Requires body close confirmation, not just wick.
I-3
Scan M15 liquidity pools. Asian session bars only. Find equal highs (BSL) and equal lows (SSL) within 3-pip tolerance. Store all pools.
I-4
Scan M5 FVGs. Last 100 bars. Store up to 20 unmitigated Fair Value Gaps with high, low, and midpoint coordinates.
I-5
Calculate M5 ATR(14). Store as SESSION_ATR and compute 5-bar average for dynamic comparison.
I-6
Reset counters. DAILY_LOSS_USED = 0, TRADES_TODAY = 0. Carry forward CONSECUTIVE_LOSSES from prior session.

Live Tick Evaluation (every tick)

T-1
Session check. Current GMT time must be within 07:00–09:30 OR 12:00–15:30. FAIL = skip all remaining rules.
T-2
News blackout. No high-impact event within ±15 minutes. FAIL = skip.
T-3
Spread check. Current spread ≤ 25 pips. FAIL = skip. If ≥ 40 pips = close all positions + halt.
T-4
Daily limits. TRADES_TODAY < 5 AND DAILY_LOSS_USED < 3% equity. FAIL = halt for day.
T-5
Loss streak. CONSECUTIVE_LOSSES checked against thresholds. 3 losses = pause session. 5 losses = pause 24 hours.
T-6
ATR gate. M5 ATR(14) > 8 pips = full trade. 5–8 pips = A+ only. < 5 pips = FAIL, all entries blocked.
T-7
BOS direction. BOS_DIRECTION must be BULL (for long) or BEAR (for short). NONE = FAIL.
T-8
Sweep detection. Wick through pool by 2–15 pips, body closes back inside. Record SWEEP_CONFIRMED + wick extreme.
T-9
FVG alignment. Unmitigated FVG must exist within 20 pips of swept pool. FAIL = reject setup.
T-10
CHoCH check. Post-sweep M1 must break swing reference in trade direction. Must occur within 5 M1 candles. Else invalidate.
T-11
Momentum delta. 2 of last 3 M1 candles must close in trade direction (body, not wick). FAIL = reject.
T-12
Confluence count. Minimum 4 of 5 pillars confirmed. All 5 = A+ entry at 1% risk. Under 4 = FAIL.
T-13
SL envelope. Structural SL must be 8–20 pips from entry. Calculate lot size from equity × risk% ÷ (SL pips × pip value).
T-14
Execute. Send 3 sub-positions via OrderSendAsync(). Mark pool consumed. Mark FVG mitigated. Reset sweep state.

Trade Management (every tick while in trade)

M-1
TP1 hit. Move SL to entry + 1 pip. Close 40% of position. Trade is now risk-free.
M-2
TP2 hit. Close 35% of position. Activate 10-pip trailing stop on remaining 25% runner.
M-3
Time exit. If trade open > 45 minutes without TP1 hit, close entire position at market.
M-4
Invalidation exit (immediate). Counter-BOS on M5 | Spread ≥ 40 pips | Price re-enters swept pool | Unscheduled news event.
M-5
Post-trade logging. Record WIN/LOSS/BE. Update streak counter. Un-tag consumed FVG and liquidity pool.
